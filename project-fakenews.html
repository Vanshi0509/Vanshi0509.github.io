Fake News Detection with XAI

Making AI accountable.

The "Black Box" Problem

While many AI models can detect fake news, they often act like "black boxes," making it impossible to understand why they reached a certain conclusion. This lack of transparency erodes trust and slows adoption. This project aimed to build a high-accuracy fake news detection model that was also transparent and interpretable, using Explainable AI (XAI) techniques.

Methodology

  • Trained a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) model on a large dataset of over 71,000 news articles.
  • Utilized LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) to make the model's predictions transparent.
  • LIME highlights the specific words or phrases within an article that most influenced the model's decision to classify it as real or fake.
  • This approach combines high performance with the accountability needed for real-world applications in journalism and policy.

Key Outcome

The LSTM model significantly outperformed the baseline, increasing detection accuracy from:

78%

(Baseline)

→

87%

(LSTM with XAI)
